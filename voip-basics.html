What is VoIP?

Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) is a technology that allows you to make voice calls using a broadband Internet connection instead of a regular (or analog) phone line.

How VoIP Works

VoIP converts your voice into digital signals that travel over the Internet. When you call a regular phone number, the signal is converted to a regular telephone signal before it reaches the destination.

  • Voice is converted to digital packets
  • Packets travel over the Internet
  • Packets are reassembled at destination
  • Digital signal converted back to voice

Traditional vs VoIP

Traditional phone systems use circuit-switched networks, while VoIP uses packet-switched networks. This fundamental difference enables VoIP's cost savings and flexibility.

Circuit-Switched: Dedicated connection for entire call duration
Packet-Switched: Data broken into packets, sent independently, reassembled

Key VoIP Terminology

Understanding common VoIP terms will help you make informed decisions about your communication system.

SIP (Session Initiation Protocol)

The signaling protocol used to establish, maintain, and terminate VoIP calls. Think of it as the "language" VoIP devices use to communicate.

Codec

Software that compresses and decompresses voice data. Common codecs include G.711 (high quality) and G.729 (bandwidth efficient).

PBX (Private Branch Exchange)

A private telephone network within a company. Cloud PBX is hosted remotely, while on-premise PBX is located at your office.

SIP Trunking

A method of delivering telephone services over the internet, connecting your PBX to the Public Switched Telephone Network (PSTN).

DID (Direct Inward Dialing)

A phone number that allows external callers to reach an internal extension directly without going through a receptionist or auto-attendant.

QoS (Quality of Service)

Network technology that prioritizes voice traffic over other types of data to ensure clear, uninterrupted calls.

IVR (Interactive Voice Response)

Automated phone system that interacts with callers, gathers information, and routes calls to the appropriate recipient.

PSTN (Public Switched Telephone Network)

The traditional circuit-switched telephone network that has been in use for over a century.

Latency

The delay between when a voice packet is sent and when it's received. Low latency is crucial for natural conversation.
