Acceptable Use Policy

Last updated: January 19, 2026

This policy outlines the acceptable use of Voiro's services and network resources.

1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") governs the use of Voiro's services and network resources. By using our services, you agree to comply with this AUP. Violation of this policy may result in suspension or termination of your services.

2. Prohibited Activities

You may not use our services to engage in, or encourage others to engage in, any of the following activities:

2.1 Illegal Activities

  • Violating any applicable local, state, national, or international law or regulation
  • Transmitting, distributing, or storing material that violates export control laws
  • Engaging in fraud, money laundering, or other financial crimes
  • Infringing upon intellectual property rights

2.2 Network Abuse

  • Attempting to gain unauthorized access to any system or network
  • Interfering with service to any user, host, or network
  • Distributing viruses, worms, Trojan horses, or other malicious code
  • Conducting denial-of-service attacks
  • Port scanning or network probing without authorization

2.3 Communications Abuse

  • Sending unsolicited bulk messages (spam)
  • Making fraudulent or misleading communications
  • Harassing, threatening, or abusive communications
  • Caller ID spoofing or manipulation
  • Auto-dialing or predictive dialing without proper consent

2.4 Content Restrictions

  • Transmitting obscene, pornographic, or sexually explicit material
  • Promoting violence, hate speech, or discrimination
  • Distributing material that is defamatory or libelous
  • Sharing confidential or proprietary information without authorization

3. Fair Usage Policy

While we do not impose strict usage limits, we expect customers to use our services reasonably. Excessive usage that negatively impacts network performance or other customers may result in service limitations or additional charges.

4. Emergency Services

Our services may support emergency calling in certain regions. However, you acknowledge that:

  • Emergency services may not be available in all areas
  • You must provide accurate location information for emergency services
  • Service interruptions may affect emergency calling capabilities
  • You should maintain alternative means of contacting emergency services

5. Monitoring and Enforcement

We reserve the right to monitor network traffic and usage patterns to ensure compliance with this AUP. We may take any action we deem appropriate in response to violations, including:

  • Issuing warnings
  • Suspending services
  • Terminating accounts
  • Reporting violations to law enforcement
